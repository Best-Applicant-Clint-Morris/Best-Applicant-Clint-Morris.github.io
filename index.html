Understanding Multi-vehicle Collision Patterns on Freeways – A Machine Learning Approach

Understanding crash patterns and underlying factors is critical to develop effective strategies and countermeasures to reduce one of the world’s leading causes of death. Given the complexity of crash mechanism and associated heterogeneity, classic statistical approaches, which lack versatility, might not be sufficient for analyzing data that is manifested in a multitude of feature dimensions. As richer data sources become available, machine learning (ML) emerges as the preferred technique. In this study, two modern ML techniques: Linear Discriminate Analysis (LDA) and Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost), were explored to classify three major types of multi-vehicle crashes (i.e., rear end, same-direction sideswipe (SDS), and angle) occurred on Interstate 285 in Georgia. It was discovered, higher speed, dark-lighted conditions, higher precipitation rate and wet surface are leading factors for angle crashes. Rear-end crashes mostly occurred in heavier traffic conditions during the daytime. SDS collisions are associated with higher speed, larger road curvature and occurred more often within interchanges, in gusty and dark-lighted conditions. While examining driver factors, rear-end collisions are mostly due to following too closely, being distracted, or improper braking. Improper lane changing is the leading factor for SDS collisions while angle collisions are commonly associated with misjudged clearance, DUI and speeding.
